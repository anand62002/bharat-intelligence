## YOUR TASK

Conduct a rigorous bull-bear debate covering all material risks and opportunities. Then produce a final investment recommendation.

**Bull Case** — Identify exactly 3 distinct reasons to be optimistic. Each point must be specific, data-grounded (reference agent signals/scores), and relevant to the 3–9 month horizon.

**Bear Case** — Identify exactly 3 distinct risks or concerns. Each must be specific and cite evidence from the agent outputs above.

**Synthesis** — One paragraph (2–4 sentences) that weighs the bull and bear cases, acknowledges key uncertainties, and justifies the final action. The synthesis paragraph MUST include a brief market-constraints note at the end: (a) the applicable NSE circuit-breaker band — ±5% for large-cap NIFTY 50/100 index constituents, ±10% for most NSE EQ stocks, ±20% for small-cap/SME/T-group; (b) a one-word liquidity assessment (LIQUID / THIN / ILLIQUID) based on average daily trading volume; (c) the appropriate position-size tier for this market-cap segment (FULL for large-cap, HALF for mid-cap, QUARTER for small-cap < ₹2 000 Cr); (d) any known upcoming ex-dividend or corporate-action dates that would affect T+2 settlement timing of the entry order.

**Final Recommendation** — Based on the debate and composite score, provide:
- action: BUY (strong conviction, upside ≥25%), HOLD (neutral, wait for clearer signal), SELL (deteriorating fundamentals/trend), or AVOID (high risk, capital preservation)
- confidence: 0–100 (your overall conviction in this call, considering data quality and signal consensus)
- risk_score: 0–100 (higher = riskier; consider volatility, debt, promoter pledging, macro headwinds)
- entry_low, entry_high: suggested buy zone in ₹ (null if SELL/AVOID or current price is fair entry)
- target: 12-month price target in ₹ (null if SELL/AVOID)
- stoploss: recommended stoploss in ₹ (for risk management)
- horizon_days: recommended holding period in days (90/180/270/365)
- upside_pct: expected percentage upside from current price to target
- upside_confidence: 0–100 confidence in the upside_pct estimate
- danger_drop_pct: maximum expected downside if the bear case materialises (positive number, e.g. 20 means 20% drawdown)
- danger_confidence: 0–100 confidence in the danger_drop_pct estimate
- market_constraints: one sentence covering the four required items — e.g. "Circuit: ±10% NSE band; Liquidity: LIQUID (avg ~5L shares/day); Position: HALF tier (mid-cap ₹8 500 Cr); Settlement: No ex-dates in next 10 days."
- headline: one crisp sentence (≤100 chars) summarising the call — e.g. "BUY: HDFC Bank at inflection, 32% upside over 9 months"

### Calibration guidelines

| Composite score | Typical action | Confidence range |
|-----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| ≥ 72            | BUY           | 65–90           |
| 55–71           | HOLD          | 50–70           |
| 40–54           | SELL          | 45–65           |
| < 40            | AVOID         | 60–85           |

Use judgment — a high composite score with conflicting signals should lower confidence. A low score with a single agent flagging extreme danger should raise risk_score significantly.

For Indian equities, consider:
- Promoter pledging > 30% is a governance red flag → raise risk_score by 10–15 points
- FII net buying is a positive tailwind → can raise confidence by 5 points
- India VIX > 20 signals elevated market risk → raise risk_score and widen entry zones
- Revenue growth > 20% YoY justifies elevated P/E for growth stocks
- Debt/equity > 1.5 in cyclical sectors warrants caution
